# Market Research — Indian Payroll Web App

**Author:** Suyash · **Date:** 2026-06-09 · **Project:** payroll-webapp

## 1. Market Size & Growth

- India payroll **services** market: **~USD 1.78B (2025) → ~USD 1.91B (2026)**, projected **~USD 2.74B by 2031 at ~7.4% CAGR**.
- **SMB segment is the fastest-growing slice — ~16.4% CAGR to 2031** as cloud SaaS removes onboarding friction. This is precisely our 50–500-employee wedge.
- Adoption: **~67% of Indian companies used payroll automation in 2024, expected to cross 85%.** Headroom remains, but the category is maturing — differentiation matters more than parity.
- Government-led digitization (real-time statutory filings, UPI rails) is **raising the compliance bar**, pushing firms off spreadsheets toward cloud platforms.

**Implementation time** (a real switching-cost signal): SMB tools (RazorpayX, Zoho, greytHR) go live in **days–2 weeks**; mid-market HRMS (Keka, HROne) **3–6 weeks**; enterprise **6–12 weeks**.

## 3. Competitive Gap → Our Wedge

Every incumbent competes on **calculation + compliance + disbursal** — the math is commoditized. **None own the upstream data supply chain** (late exits, silent CTC changes, stale attendance sync). This validates the PLAN.md thesis: *payroll as a continuously-reconciled readiness score, not a monthly fire drill.* Our differentiation (Readiness Score, Data Freshness Vitals, Change Handshake, Lifecycle Clock, Pre-Flight Checklist) sits in whitespace the market treats as "someone else's problem."

## 4. Positioning Implications

- **Table-stakes floor we must hit to be credible:** statutory auto-compliance (PF/ESI/TDS/PT), payslips, multi-state, biometric/HRMS integration, fast disbursal, days-not-weeks onboarding.
- **Price anchor:** ₹40–150/emp/mo is the established band; the readiness-score moat can justify the upper end if it demonstrably prevents errors.
- **Beachhead:** 50–500-emp firms — too complex for Zoho/RazorpayX simplicity, too cost-sensitive for Darwinbox, currently settling for Keka/greytHR which ignore the supply-chain problem.
